Pillars

A Yosemite-inspired restraint: fewer elements, each one considered. The goal is stability, warmth, and endurance.

Wholeness

No inner split. Your feeling, your words, and your actions tell the same story.

Breadth

Perception widens. You can hold multiple truths without losing the thread.

Telos

Purpose returns. Each step asks: what is this for, and what does it serve?

Tending

Endurance over intensity. Protect the ember so the fire lasts.

Signal

What’s true and alive. Felt before it is named. Never forced.

Restraint

We subtract what distorts. Scale is earned. Quiet carries farther than noise.

Models

Simple maps that reduce confusion. Use them. Change them. Keep only what clarifies.

Three-system alignment

Feeling (what matters) + Meaning (what it means) + Motion (what happens next). Misalignment creates overwhelm, stagnation, or emptiness.

Practices

Small rituals that keep wattage clean. No optimization. Stewardship.

Daily (10–30 min)

1) Settle the system (breath + body).
2) Check Feeling / Meaning / Motion.
3) Do one clean act. Stop before depletion.

Weekly (30–60 min)

Review wattage leaks. Name the signal. Remove friction. Adjust pace — not ambition.

The stop rule

When you feel performance rising, stop. Rest protects wattage. Stopping early keeps the ember alive.
